## SBC4

- `Tease:` The core user is choosing tone in a message right now.
- `Lede:` FetchMoji serves natural-language, multilingual, privacy-conscious writers; developer interest is secondary.
- `Why it matters:` A narrow repeated job keeps product and distribution choices honest.
- `Go deeper:` Compare the primary audience, important segments, and explicit exclusions.

## Primary audience

FetchMoji is for people who are writing a message and know the meaning they want
to convey but cannot confidently choose the emoji.

Common situations include:

- adding warmth without overstatement;
- acknowledging awkwardness or secondhand embarrassment;
- choosing between playful, supportive, formal, and sarcastic tones;
- searching in a natural phrase instead of an English keyboard label;
- checking a choice before sending it in work, dating, family, or group chat.

The repeated job is small and immediate. The product succeeds when the person
gets a trustworthy choice, copies it, and returns the next time the same
uncertainty appears.

## Important segments

- **Multilingual searchers** use reviewed phrase pages and search examples in
  the language they actually write.
- **Privacy-conscious users** want message-adjacent text to stay on the device.
- **Frequent writers** benefit from a browser shortcut that removes the trip to
  a separate website.
- **Developers and language researchers** are a secondary audience for the
  methodology, retrieval benchmark, and client-side architecture.

## Not the primary audience

FetchMoji is not designed first for people browsing a comprehensive Unicode
dictionary, collecting decorative emoji art, or generating custom images. Those
needs are served by different products and should not pull the core experience
away from contextual selection.
